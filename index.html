CRIMES AGAINST NATIONAL SECURITY AND THE LAW OF NATIONS		 Art. 114 Treason
Any Filipino citizen who levies war against the Philippines or adheres to her enemies, giving them aid or comfort within the Philippines or elsewhere.
1. Offender is a Filipino citizen or resident alien; 2. There is a war in which the Philippines is involved; 3. Offender either: (a) levies war against the Government, or (b) adheres to enemies, giving them aid or comfort
 Punishable by reclusion perpetua to death; two-witness rule — each overt act must be witnessed by at least 2 witnesses
A judicial confession of the accused suffices in lieu of two-witness rule
Extrajudicial confession alone is NOT sufficient (must be corroborated)
WAR: War must exist; Adherence to enemy or Acts of levying war; Resident alien or Filipino
Doctrine of Allegiance (Laurel v. Misa, 77 Phil 856): Filipino's allegiance is not suspended by enemy occupation; two-witness rule is a substantive requirement, not merely procedural
RA 11479 (Anti-Terrorism Act of 2020), Sec. 9: Terrorism distinct from treason but may overlap

CRIMES AGAINST NATIONAL SECURITY AND THE LAW OF NATIONS		 Art. 122 Piracy in General and Mutiny on the High Seas
Persons who shall attack or seize a vessel on the high seas or Philippine waters, or who seize the whole or part of cargo, its equipment, or personal belongings of complement or passengers.
Piracy: 1. Vessel is on high seas or Philippine waters; 2. Offenders are NOT members of its complement or passengers; 3. Attack or seizure by violence/intimidation or force upon things. Mutiny: 1. Offenders are members of the crew; 2. They unlawfully resist a lawful order or seize vessel
 Traditional piracy = outsiders (not passengers or crew); committed on high seas or Philippine waters
PD 532 (Anti-Piracy and Anti-Highway Robbery Law) expanded piracy to include passengers and crew members in Philippine waters
Under PD 532, if offenders are passengers or crew, the crime is still piracy (not robbery or mutiny)
OUTSIDER: Offense on high seas; Under PD 532 expanded; They are not crew/passengers (RPC); Seizure or attack; Inside Philippine waters (PD 532); Distinction from robbery
Piracy is a crime against humanity (hostis humani generis); universal jurisdiction applies; under the RPC, offenders must be outsiders — if crew or passengers commit the act, it may be robbery; PD 532 eliminated the outsider requirement
PD 532 (Anti-Piracy and Anti-Highway Robbery Law of 1974); RA 9372 (Human Security Act)

CRIMES AGAINST PUBLIC ORDER		 Art. 134/134-A Rebellion and Coup d'État
Art. 134 – Rebellion: Rising publicly and taking up arms against the Government for any of the purposes listed (PURE). Art. 134-A – Coup d'État: A swift attack against duly constituted authorities by military, PNP, paramilitary, or private sector personnel.
Rebellion: 1. Public uprising; 2. Taking arms against the Government; 3. Purpose: remove allegiance to Government, deprive Chief Executive/Congress of powers, or inflict acts of hate/revenge on government/members. Coup d'État: 1. Swift attack; 2. By military, PNP, paramilitary, or private persons; 3. Against duly constituted government authorities, military, police, communication networks; 4. Seize power/diminish authority
 Rebellion absorbs all common crimes committed in furtherance thereof (doctrine of absorption, People v. Hernandez; Lagman v. Medialdea 2017)
Terrorism is NOT absorbed by rebellion (RA 11479 Sec. 49); crimes NOT in furtherance of rebellion are separate
Murder for personal reasons during rebellion = separate crime not absorbed
PURE: Political purpose (remove allegiance); Uprising publicly; Remove powers from officials; Engage government forces. SWIFT: Swift attack; Without warning; Involves military/PNP; Force and seizure; Taking of authority
People v. Hernandez (GR L-6025, 1956): rebellion absorbs all crimes committed in furtherance thereof; Lagman v. Medialdea (GR 231658, 2017): Hernandez doctrine reaffirmed; Gonzales v. Abaya: coup d'état also absorbs related offenses; NO absorption doctrine in sedition (Art. 139)

A libel is a public and malicious imputation of a crime, or of a vice or defect, real or imaginary, or any act, omission, condition, status, or circumstance tending to cause the dishonor, discredit, or contempt of a natural or juridical person, or to blacken the memory of one who is dead.

Privileged communications (Art. 354): (a) private communications from moral duty; (b) fair and true report of official proceedings; (c) fair commentaries on public matters of public interest
